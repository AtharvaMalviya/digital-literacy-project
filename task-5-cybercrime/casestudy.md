CASE STUDY: IDENTITY THEFT

Identity theft is a type of cybercrime where someone uses another person’s identity or personal information without permission, usually to gain financial benefits or deceive others. This can include using someone’s social media account, phone number, or even creating fake profiles.

In my experience, a close friend’s social media account was hacked and used to scam others. The attacker gained access to the account, possibly through a phishing link or weak password. Once inside, they started sending messages to people in the friend’s contact list, pretending to be them. The messages were urgent in tone, asking for money and giving reasons like an emergency or immediate need.

Since the messages came from a trusted account, many people believed it was genuine. A few of my friends actually transferred money without verifying properly. Later, it was discovered that the account had been compromised, and the real owner had no idea what was happening.

Students and young users are often targeted in such cases because they may not be very cautious about account security. The consequences can include financial loss, damaged trust, and emotional stress.

This incident highlights the importance of strong passwords, enabling two-factor authentication, and always verifying unexpected requests, even if they come from someone you know.
